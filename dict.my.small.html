modicum
1. a small quantity of a particular thing, especially something desirable or valuable. "his statement had a modicum of truth"


iota
1.an extremely small amount. "nothing she said seemed to make an iota of difference"


paucity
1. the presence of something in only small or insufficient quantities or amounts. "a paucity of information"


scanty
1. small or insufficient in quantity or amount. "they paid whatever they could out of their scanty wages to their families"


scarcity
1.the state of being scarce or in short supply; shortage. "a time of scarcity"


dearth
1.a scarcity or lack of something. "there is a dearth of evidence"


sparse
1. thinly dispersed or scattered. "areas of sparse population"
2. in short supply; scanty. "information on earnings is sparse"


rarity
1. the state or quality of being rare. "the rarity of the condition"


meager
1. lacking in quantity or quality. thin "they were forced to supplement their meager earnings"


scraggy
1. thin and bony. "scraggy mongrels"


sporadic
1. occurring at irregular intervals or only in a few places; scattered or isolated. "sporadic fighting broke out"


slight
1. small in degree; inconsiderable. "a slight increase in temperature." "a slight romantic comedy"
2. (of a person or their build) not sturdy; thin or slender. "she was slight and delicate-looking"


deficit
1. the amount by which something, especially a sum of money, is too small.
